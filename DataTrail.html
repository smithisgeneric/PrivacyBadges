Personal Information: Data Trail Timeline

Made by the Hive Toronto .

Learners will create a timeline video or slideshow to demonstrate how your information gets collected by companies and other organizations throughout the course of a typical day.

Learners can earn the Date Trail Timeline Badge.

  • 5
    min

    Preparation

    Print out Legit-O-Meter Worksheets

    Review the definitions of personal information and personally identifiable information from the teaching kit homepage

  • 10
    min

    Introduction Round: 3 Communication Activities

    Consider 3 ways you communicate with other people in a typical day. If you are alone you can write down 3 ideas on a piece of paper. If you are in a group, have a few volunteers each share an idea verbally.

    Next, consider whether any of your personal information gets collected by companies or other organizations in the course of these 3 ways of communicating.

    For the purposes of this activity, your personal information can include:
    • your photo or video image
    • your name or username
    • the content of your communications (i.e., emails, texts)
    • any numbers associated with your identity (i.e. phone number, account number, etc.).

  • 1 hour
    min

    Produce a Data Trail Timeline

    To begin to produce your own data trail timeline, first spend some time writing down entries that examine where your information was collected by companies in the course of a day (approximately 1 per hour). After you have a timeline of 5-10 entries, find audiovisual assets that are under Creative Commons (CC) or openly licensed to use to tell your data trail timeline story.

    If you are new to Creative Commons (CC) and openly licensed assets review this. You can search for Creative Commons assets at the CC Search page.

    Tell your data trail timeline story in an audiovisual way. Options to create your data trail timeline story are:
    • Create a video remix using Mozilla Popcorn. Begin by reviewing the online help information for Popcorn and then remix the template data trail timeline [add].
    • Create a slide show using Google Slides (part of Google Docs) or another program of your choice. If you are new to Google Slides you may wish to review Getting Started with Google Slides and then remix the template slide presentation.

    15
    min

    Reflect and Review

    Reflect on what you or the group have learned. To reflect and review, we suggest you screen projects within or group or share with a peer. You can use the following discussion prompts.

    • Was there anything that surprised you from your data timeline, or other timelines you viewed?
    • Are there times in a typical day when you cannot opt out of having your information collected?
    • Are you recorded on video or by surveillance cameras in a typical day?
    • What countries does the data and personal information travel to?
    • You've created a data trail timeline but is privacy important to you? What issues do you care about?
